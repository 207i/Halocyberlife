Biophotonics · Research Concept

Lumen Extraction TRIA-CORE™

A visual and conceptual exploration of how ultraweak biological light can be retained, redirected, and studied by engineering the environment photons move through— rather than attempting to chase them after they escape.

View Patent Specification ↗

The Core Insight

Most biophotonic measurement systems fail not because photons are too weak, but because they are allowed to escape. TRIA-CORE™ approaches the problem differently: instead of chasing photons, it reshapes the environment so photons are statistically retained.

Functional System Concepts

Biophoton Emission Context

Biological tissues emit ultraweak light as a natural byproduct of metabolic activity. These emissions are continuous, low-level, and highly sensitive to physiological state.

Ultraweak Emission

Non-Invasive Health Monitoring

Changes in biophoton flux and spectral distribution can indicate stress, injury, or metabolic shifts—offering a way to observe biological health without physical contact or chemical markers.

Biological Insight

Micro-Scale Energy Recovery

When photons are sufficiently concentrated, a portion of the retained flux may be converted into usable micro-energy—suitable for sensing, buffering, or low-power experimental applications.

Micro-Energy

Precision Photon Analysis

Concentrated photon streams enable improved signal-to-noise ratios, making it possible to analyze temporal patterns, intensity variation, and spectral structure previously lost to environmental noise.
