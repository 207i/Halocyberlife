BioLumen™

TRIA-CORE™ Inward-Directed Biophotonic Extraction System

System Documentation ↗ View Architecture
Interface: TRIA-CORE™ Cycle: Continuous Module: Biophotonic Extraction

BioLumen™ is a biophotonic extraction architecture designed to capture ultra-weak photon emissions from biological material using inward-directed optical geometry. At its core, the TRIA-CORE™ system compresses, redirects, and stabilizes naturally occurring light into a controllable photonic output—operating as a continuous process rather than a momentary event.

Biophotonic Harvesting Core

Inward-directed TRIA-CORE™ collector geometry captures ultraweak photon emissions from biological material, redirecting lateral and scattered light into a centralized optical pathway for amplification, sensing, or downstream conversion.

Photon extraction

Ultra-low profile capture surfaces

Thin, planar collection surfaces integrate TRIA-CORE™ geometries and reflective layers, enabling biophoton interception and redirection without bulky housings or protruding hardware.

Low-profile collectors

Emergency + redundancy

Surfaces with multiple excitation methods—if one fails, another still triggers visibility.

Failsafe modes

Dual-mode biophotonic stimulation

Electromagnetic and acoustic excitation operate independently or in combination to stimulate biological material, enabling controlled enhancement of biophoton emission across varying conditions and system states.

Multi-modal excitation

Photon sensing & feedback

Integrated photonic sensors measure intensity, temporal variation, and spectral characteristics of harvested biophotons, enabling real-time feedback, system tuning, and correlation with biological metabolic states.

Photon sensing

Modular system integration

BioLumen™ components are designed as modular units that may operate independently or as part of larger arrays, enabling scalable deployment, parallel photon harvesting, and integration with external analytical, control, or energy systems.

Modular architecture
