Title of the Invention

BioLumen™ — Biological Photonic Extraction and Multi-Stage Hybrid Energy Conversion System

Field of the Invention

This invention relates to renewable energy systems, ultra-low-light optical extraction, quantum-gain amplification, bio-electronic interfaces, photonic collection manifolds, hybrid biomass/thermal energy modules, and energy-storage regulation systems. Specifically, it involves the extraction, amplification, and conversion of biophotons emitted from biological organisms into usable electrical power.

Background of the Invention

Biological organisms (plants, fungi, microbes) emit extremely low-level light (biophotons) through metabolic and oxidative reactions:

  • Continuous or semi-continuous
  • Extremely low-intensity (single-photon level)
  • Wavelengths ~350–900 nm

No existing technology can:

  • Extract and amplify biophotons into usable electrical energy
  • Integrate optical, thermal, and gasifier subsystems
  • Use non-destructive frequency stimulation to enhance photon output
  • Multiply ultraweak signals via a quantum-gain resonator

Detailed Description of the Invention

1. Biophoton Chamber (FIG. 2)

Sealed enclosure with optical blackening, collector walls, plant tray, detector windows, Y-axis actuator, and embedded reflective surfaces.

2. Photonic Extraction Array (FIG. 3)

Arrays of 8–128 APDs, PMTs, filters (350–900 nm), micro-lenses linked to quantum amplifiers.

3. Fiber-Optic Photonic Funnel (FIG. 3 & 10)

1–5 inch wide funnel with mirrored walls, fiber bundles, polished coupling. Concentrates scattered photons efficiently.

4. Actuated Optical Collector (FIG. 11)

Y-axis moving head sweeps across chamber to dynamically change angle and increase temporal capture.

5. Frequency-Stimulation Enhancement (FIG. 9)

EM field pulses plant to enhance metabolic photon emissions non-destructively.

6. Quantum-Gain Resonator Chain (FIG. 4)

Pre-amplifier → resonator layers → noise gate → phase combiner → optical-to-electrical unit.

7. Supercapacitor Ladder Storage (FIG. 12)

Stores micro-energy via supercaps, controller, and surge protection inverter.

8. Biomass Hybrid Boost (FIG. 7)

Optional gasifier chamber + heat exchanger + TEG + ash outlet.

9. Output Regulation (FIG. 7)

5V USB, 12V DC, AC outputs with protection and inversion.

Abstract

The BioLumen™ system introduces a new renewable energy category using biological photon emissions. The system extracts biophotons from plants via photodiode arrays, fiber-optic funnels, and actuated optical collectors. It amplifies photon signals using a quantum-gain resonator and converts them into electrical charge stored in a supercapacitor ladder. Frequency-stimulation modules enhance photon output. Optional biomass + thermoelectric stages provide hybrid energy options. The result is the world's first regulated biological photonic energy generator.
