Ocean Generator™ v2

Modular near‑shore seawater evaporation and condensation system — patent pending.

View Patent Documentation ↗
Core idea: solar‑thermal freshwater tech Goal: scalable modular system Focus: safe & eco‑friendly

The Ocean Generator™ v2 is a modular freshwater generation system that uses solar‑thermal energy to evaporate and condense seawater near shore. Designed for simplicity, safety, and scalable deployment, it provides potable water without high‑pressure membranes or complex mechanical systems.

System Overview

This architecture uses segmented solar concentrators, modular platforms, and passive fail‑safe mechanisms to create a reliable freshwater source using abundant solar energy.

Modules & Components

Seawater Intake

Passive screened intake with low‑pressure flow to protect local ecology.

Solar‑Thermal Evaporation

Segmented solar arrays with shutters for fail‑safe heat control.

Condensation Module

Steam condenses to clean freshwater in sealed collectors.

Thermal Safety Control

Automatic shutter & temperature sensors ensure safe operation.

Modular Platform

Surface for shallow near‑shore deployment with corrosion resistance.

Control & Automation

Monitors status & executes safe modes automatically.

Isolation Architecture

Sealed conduits protect electrical systems from seawater.

Ocean Generator™ v2 represents a scalable, efficient, and safe approach to freshwater production. With solar‑thermal energy as the core driver, it avoids complex infrastructure and enables decentralized deployment.
